## 1. Recommendation in one paragraph

Build a dedicated Fire Risk Assessment service page at `/services/fire-risk-assessments`, then support it with a small, tightly linked cluster of resource articles and a set of town and borough location pages. All four research reports independently reached the same architectural conclusion (a dedicated page plus a supporting cluster, not a buried sub-section). The single most defensible commercial angle, and the one no competitor in the catchment owns, is the post-assessment remedials path: J&L arranges the assessment through an accredited contractor and then carries out the resulting fire alarm, emergency lighting and electrical testing work directly. That is the message the page architecture should be built around.

## 2. How the four reports were reconciled

The reports agree on direction and disagree on numbers. Where they conflicted, the following rules were applied:

Keyword volumes: three of the four reports state explicitly that they could not access tool-verified volume data and gave directional estimates. The fourth ("Market & SEO Research Report") cites point figures attributed to a "SimilarWeb API" but those figures are not corroborated by the other three and are in places lower than the consensus. All volumes below are therefore treated as estimates, and the consensus figure leans to the conservative (lower) end of the overlapping ranges. A one-off Ahrefs, Semrush or Google Keyword Planner pull is recommended before any copywriting or paid-media budget is committed. This does not block page-architecture decisions, which the data is robust enough to support.

Regulatory facts: dates were cross-checked against primary and authoritative sources because they will appear on a live client page. One report stated the Fire Safety (England) Regulations 2022 came into force on 26 January 2023; this is incorrect. The verified date is 23 January 2023. Verified facts are in Section 11.

Architecture: unanimous across all four reports. Option B (dedicated page plus supporting cluster) is adopted.

## 4. SERP and competitor synthesis

The Essex SERP is less saturated than London and is winnable. London is harder but, critically, no fire-and-security installer currently ranks in the top 10 for "fire risk assessment London"; that page is held by FRA-specialist consultancies. Across all priority terms the results are a mix of national directories (Checkatrade, Bark, Yell), municipal and fire-service informational pages (Essex County Fire and Rescue), and regional specialist service pages. Top-ranking specialist pages run roughly 1,200 to 2,500 words with a 6 to 10 question FAQ block and explicit accreditation callouts.

Direct comparators (fire-and-security installers who have added FRA, in J&L's catchment):

| Competitor | Model | FRA page structure | Accreditations | Read for J&L |

Specialist consultancies (Essex Fire Safety, DC2 Fire, FireRA, Mast Safety, IQ Fire Solutions, Compliance Fire, Safe I.S., JGS) deliver the assessment but explicitly do not carry out remedial works. National providers and aggregators hold tail and near-me rankings through programmatic local pages.

J&L's accreditation stack (SSAIB, CHAS, FIA, BAFE) is competitive with all direct comparators and stronger than half. The fully accredited assessment-plus-remedials offer under one provider is rare; only AJ Fire and Firstford cleanly market the full loop. That is J&L's positioning lane.

## 5. Competitor gaps to exploit

Ranked by commercial value to J&L:

1. Post-FRA remedials. Consultancies distance themselves from remedials; installers treat assessments as a minor lead source. Almost no one writes content for the step after the report is delivered. This is J&L's natural territory because it actually does the work.
2. Conveyancing and flat sales. Owned by conveyancing solicitors and property portals, not by any service provider. The single clearest content gap in the wider market. High urgency (stalled property chains), low competition.
3. Greater London borough-level targeting (Havering, Redbridge, Barking, Newham, Waltham Forest). Almost no fire-and-security installer targets these despite them sitting inside J&L's catchment.
4. Town-level Essex pages with genuine local content. Most competitors run one Essex-level page; only FireRA and MD Fire have town pages and theirs are template-thin.
5. Specific unanswered questions: who pays for the FRA when selling a flat; the difference between an EWS1 form and an FRA; what grade of alarm an HMO needs under BS 5839-6 versus BS 5839-1; what post-FRA remedial work costs.

## 6. Recommended architecture (Option B), mapped to this site

The site is Next.js 15 with services driven by `lib/data.ts` and rendered through `app/services/[service]/page.tsx`. The FRA service should follow the exact existing pattern so it inherits the established layout, schema and internal-link behaviour.

```
/services/fire-risk-assessments        [pillar service page, ~2,000 to 2,500 words]
/blog/...                              [supporting resource cluster, 4 to 6 posts]
/locations/... and /[service]/[location]  [town and borough relevance]
```

A standalone child-page tree (for example `/services/fire-risk-assessments/landlords-hmo`) was proposed by one report. It is not recommended for the first build. The existing site has no nested service children and uses the blog plus the service-location matrix to capture long-tail intent. Using the blog cluster for audience and conveyancing intent, rather than inventing a new nested URL pattern, keeps the build consistent with the codebase and avoids thin near-duplicate pages. Nested children can be revisited later if the blog cluster underperforms.

## 11. Verified regulatory facts for use in copy

Verified against authoritative and primary sources on 23 May 2026. Use these exact framings:

- Regulatory Reform (Fire Safety) Order 2005: the foundational duty. The responsible person for any non-domestic premises, and for the common parts of multi-occupied residential buildings, must carry out and maintain a suitable and sufficient fire risk assessment.
- Fire Safety Act 2021: clarified that the Order covers the structure, external walls (including cladding, balconies and windows) and individual flat entrance doors of multi-occupied residential buildings.
- Fire Safety (England) Regulations 2022: in force 23 January 2023 (not 26 January). Additional duties for multi-occupied residential buildings, with extra duties above 11 metres and above 18 metres.
- Building Safety Act 2022, Section 156: effective 1 October 2023. Removed the previous five-employee threshold, so all responsible persons must now record the assessment in full; requires recording the identity of whoever undertakes or reviews it; raised fines from Level 3 (£1,000) to Level 5 (unlimited).
- Fire Safety (Residential Evacuation Plans) (England) Regulations 2025 (SI 2025/797): in force 6 April 2026. Adds residential personal emergency evacuation plan duties for higher-risk residential buildings, on top of the existing stack.
- BAFE SP205 (Life Safety Fire Risk Assessment): Version 6.0 published 7 November 2025, effective 31 March 2026, mapped against BS 8674.
- EWS1 form: a separate RICS lending-valuation document, not a life-safety assessment. Do not present an FRA as a substitute for an EWS1, or the reverse.

Copy caution: do not state as universal law that a converted-flat sale always requires an FRA. Frame it as a common lender or solicitor requirement during conveyancing. Any final legal wording should be checked against the underlying legislation before publication.

## 12. Open items requiring a client decision (Jag)

1. FRA partner accreditation. The page's credibility depends on naming the assessor's certification. Confirm the accredited contractor holds BAFE SP205 (Version 6.0) or is NFRAR Tier 3 listed. If neither, the page should be repositioned around remedials, with the assessment framed as "arranged through our accredited partner". This is a gating decision for the delivery-model copy.
2. Existing fire alarms page wording. That page currently states J&L "carries out fire risk assessments" directly. Given the assessment is delivered via a contractor, this wording should be reconciled with the new page to stay accurate and consistent.
3. Pricing display. Decide whether to publish conservative banded guide pricing (which reduces friction and answers cost queries) or to keep pricing to "quote after survey". Public 2026 market ranges for reference: single-let flat £150 to £260; small HMO £200 to £400; licensed HMO £395 to £550; small commercial £199 to £499; larger or multi-storey £600 to £1,500+; London and South East typically 20 to 40 per cent higher.
